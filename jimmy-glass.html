Jimmy Glass - The Goalkeeper Who Saved Carlisle United

Sports May 8, 1999

James Robert Glass (born 1 August 1973) is an English former professional footballer who played as a goalkeeper.

He is chiefly remembered for scoring the last-minute goal against Plymouth Argyle which kept Carlisle United in the Football League in 1999, while on loan from Swindon Town. The drama of Glass's late goal, which came in one of only three games that he played for Carlisle, has since made it famous in English football at a level beyond its immediate ramifications.

The Historic Moment

On May 8, 1999, Carlisle United faced relegation from the Football League. They needed to score in the dying moments of their final game against Plymouth Argyle to avoid dropping out of the league entirely.

In one of the most dramatic moments in football history, goalkeeper Jimmy Glass came up for a corner in stoppage time. The ball fell to him in the penalty area, and he smashed it into the net to secure a 2-1 victory.

Aftermath and Legacy

This goal preserved Carlisle's Football League status and became legendary in English football folklore. Despite this incredible moment, Glass's career never reached great heights, and he retired from professional football in 2001.

The goal has been featured in numerous football documentaries and countdowns of the greatest football moments. In 2013, Glass returned to Carlisle for a charity match commemorating the goal.
